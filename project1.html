The Design Challenge

For the "Tabletop Toy" competition, my team aimed to create a portable version of Hangman. The primary constraint was portability: all game components—the gallows, the human figure, and the board—had to be stored inside a self-contained "book" structure that could be 3D printed without assembly-heavy hardware.

My Technical Contribution: CAD & Tolerancing

I led the SolidWorks assembly and component design. This project required a deep understanding of 3D printing limitations, specifically regarding friction fits and snap-joints.

Key Engineering Features:
  • Precision Tolerancing: Implemented a 0.2mm tolerance stack-up analysis to ensure the hangman body parts stay in their "pockets" during transport but remain easily removable for play.
  • Snap-Fit Mechanics: Engineered a vertical gallows that snaps into the book's base, providing structural stability without the need for screws or glue.
  • Design for Manufacturing : Optimized the book shells for FDM printing by ensuring no overhangs exceeded 45 degrees, eliminating the need for excessive support material.

Reflections & Engineering Growth

This project was my first experience with Rapid Prototyping under a strict 48-hour deadline. It taught me how to bridge the gap between a digital CAD model and a physical prototype.

What I learned: Initially, our hinge was too rigid. I had to iterate the geometry in CAD to create a "living hinge" style pin that wouldn't snap under stress. This sharpened my ability to analyze material properties and stress points during the design phase.
